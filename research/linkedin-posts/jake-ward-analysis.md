# Jake Ward: B2B SEO Content Planning Playbook (H2 2026)

Source: [jake-ward-content-strategy.txt](./jake-ward-content-strategy.txt)

Jake Ward's core message: before planning new Q3 content, audit what you already have. Most B2B teams are sitting on quick wins in published content rather than needing an all-new plan.

## 3 Most Important Playbook Steps

### 1. Build a full content inventory with performance data

Export every blog URL into a spreadsheet (e.g. via Screaming Frog), then enrich each row with data from **Ahrefs**, **Google Analytics**, and **Google Search Console**.

This gives you a single source of truth for what exists, how it performs, and where the gaps are — the foundation for every decision that follows.

### 2. Map keywords to each important URL

For every key page, document:

- **Primary keyword**
- **Secondary keywords / variations**
- **Current rankings** for target terms

Use Google Search Console or Ahrefs. If a page ranks for nothing yet, identify the most relevant target keyword in Ahrefs and assign it.

Without keyword-to-URL mapping, you cannot spot cannibalization, prioritize rewrites, or judge whether a page has reached its potential.

### 3. Audit every post, assign an action, then prioritize

**Classify each URL** into one of five actions:

| Action   | When to use                                      |
|----------|--------------------------------------------------|
| Leave    | Already reached its potential                    |
| Merge    | Cannibalizing or too similar to another page     |
| Delete   | No longer useful or valuable                     |
| Update   | Small improvements needed                        |
| Rewrite  | Major improvements needed                        |

**Prioritize updates and rewrites** where:

- Target keywords sit in **positions 4–20** (close to page-one wins)
- The page has **higher conversion or revenue potential**
- There are **obvious improvement opportunities**
- The page **isn't yet being retrieved by LLMs**

## Key Takeaway

Not all of your content plan needs to be net-new. Ward's playbook is an inventory-first, action-oriented audit: know what you have, know what each page targets, decide what to do with it, then focus effort where rankings, revenue, and AI visibility overlap.
