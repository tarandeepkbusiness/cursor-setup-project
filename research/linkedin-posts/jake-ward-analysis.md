## Core Philosophy

Jake Ward's content planning rests on three interconnected beliefs:

1. **Audit before you create.** Most B2B teams don't need an all-new content plan — they're sitting on quick wins in content they've already published.
2. **AI search is an expansion of SEO, not a separate channel.** Stop building parallel "AEO" strategies, teams, and content silos. One integrated approach covers both.
3. **Earned beats owned for AI visibility.** Roughly **85% of AI visibility** comes from what others say about you; only **~13%** comes from your own domain. Brands are **6.5× more likely** to be cited via third-party sources than their own site, and nearly **90%** of those third-party mentions come from listicles, comparisons, and reviews.

The full playbook has two halves: optimize what you own, then earn presence where AI actually looks.

## Part 1: Owned Content Audit Playbook

*Run this before planning new content for H2 2026.*

### Step 1 — Build a full content inventory with performance data

Export every blog URL into a spreadsheet (e.g. via Screaming Frog), then enrich each row with data from **Ahrefs**, **Google Analytics**, and **Google Search Console**.

This creates a single source of truth for what exists, how it performs, and where the gaps are.

### Step 2 — Map keywords to each important URL

For every key page, document:

- **Primary keyword**
- **Secondary keywords / variations**
- **Current rankings** for target terms

Use Google Search Console or Ahrefs. If a page ranks for nothing yet, identify the most relevant target keyword in Ahrefs and assign it.

### Step 3 — Audit every post and assign an action

| Action   | When to use                                      |
|----------|--------------------------------------------------|
| Leave    | Already reached its potential                    |
| Merge    | Cannibalizing or too similar to another page     |
| Delete   | No longer useful or valuable                     |
| Update   | Small improvements needed                        |
| Rewrite  | Major improvements needed                        |

### Step 4 — Prioritize updates and rewrites

Focus effort where:

- Target keywords sit in **positions 4–20** (close to page-one wins)
- The page has **higher conversion or revenue potential**
- There are **obvious improvement opportunities**
- The page **isn't yet being retrieved by LLMs**

## Part 2: AI Visibility & Earned Media Playbook

*Treat AI discovery as earned media + owned content + intentional distribution — guided by what AI already rewards.*

### Step 5 — Research what AI is already citing

1. Open **ChatGPT** (free) or **Mentions** (paid)
2. Enter the prompts you want to appear in
3. Study which brands AI recommends
4. Export the list of cited sources
5. Find where you're mentioned — and where you're missing
6. Prioritize by the **most-cited sources**

This replaces guesswork with evidence: you're reverse-engineering what AI search already trusts.

### Step 6 — Earn presence on third-party sources

Nearly 90% of third-party AI mentions come from **listicles, comparisons, and reviews** — the "best X tools" roundups most teams neglect. Prioritize:

- **Get added** to sources you're missing from
- **Move higher** in lists you're already on
- **Create content** based on currently cited topics
- **Publish** on distribution channels like **Medium** and **LinkedIn Pulse**
- **Add natural mentions** in relevant **Reddit** threads

The goal is not a separate "AEO" content stream — it's increasing your footprint across the same sources and topics AI already surfaces.

### Step 7 — Track AI visibility across platforms

Use tools like **Mentions** to monitor where you show up (and don't) across **ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity**, and other AI platforms.

Stop treating your own site as the whole strategy. Your domain is only ~13% of the AI visibility picture.

## How the Two Halves Fit Together

| Layer | Focus | Ward's Playbook |
|-------|-------|-----------------|
| **Owned** | Your blog & site | Inventory → keyword map → audit → prioritize updates/rewrites |
| **Earned** | Third-party citations | Research AI citations → earn roundup/comparison mentions → track across platforms |
| **Integrated** | One strategy | SEO + AI visibility under one team, one plan — not parallel "AEO" |

Ward's recommended sequence:

1. **Audit existing content** (Steps 1–4) to capture quick wins on your domain.
2. **Research AI citation patterns** (Step 5) to see where visibility actually lives.
3. **Pursue earned media** (Steps 6–7) on the listicles, comparisons, and reviews AI already trusts.
4. **Run it for 90 days** and measure what shifts in both rankings and AI mentions.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Not all of your content plan needs to be net-new.** Start with the spreadsheet audit.
- **Don't silo AI search.** It's earned media, owned content, and distribution — not a separate "AEO" channel.
- **85% of AI visibility is off-site.** Roundups, comparisons, and reviews are the highest-leverage targets.
- **Prioritize where multiple signals overlap:** positions 4–20, revenue potential, LLM retrieval gaps, and missing third-party citations on high-authority listicles.
