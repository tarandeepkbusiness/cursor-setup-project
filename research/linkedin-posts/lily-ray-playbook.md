# Research Summary: Lily Ray
**Topic:** Defensive SEO, E-E-A-T, and LLM Content Visibility

## 1. The Comparison Page Trap
Lily warns against the "gold rush" of using AI to scale "Brand X vs. Brand Y" or "[Competitor] Alternatives" pages. While these are currently a "GEO goldmine," they are becoming a primary target for AI spam filters.
- **The Core Problem:** Google and AI companies (OpenAI/Perplexity) are shifting toward objective 3rd-party reviews (Reddit, G2, Trustpilot). 
- **The Requirement:** Real "proof of testing" is becoming mandatory. AI-spun comparison pages that trash competitors without authentic evidence of use are becoming long-term SEO liabilities.

## 2. The SEO-AI Mirror Effect
A critical finding from Lily’s research is the "Mirror Effect": **"If you fall in Google, you fall in AI."**
- **Citation Trajectory:** Citations in LLMs like ChatGPT often follow the same visibility trajectory as Google search rankings. 
- **The Risk:** Many "GEO hacks" recommended by new practitioners are actually bad for traditional SEO. Destroying your foundational SEO with spammy tactics will inevitably lead to a loss of AI search visibility as LLMs clean up their data sources.

## 3. Technical Visibility for LLM Crawlers
Lily highlights a major technical gap: most AI crawlers (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot) **do not execute JavaScript** when fetching content.
- **The Issue:** If a B2B SaaS site relies heavily on client-side rendering (JavaScript), the AI "brain" might see a blank page or missing metadata.
- **The Solution:** Audit raw HTML for client-side rendering issues, missing structured data, and alt text gaps to ensure content is "LLM-friendly" at a foundational level.

## 4. Actionable Playbook for B2B SaaS (Defensive SEO)
1. **Prioritize Authenticity over Scale:** Only build comparison pages where you can provide genuine, evidence-backed reviews.
2. **Audit Technical Visibility:** Ensure your most valuable content is readable by bots in the raw HTML, without requiring JavaScript execution.
3. **Monitor the Google-AI Connection:** Use traditional SEO health as a leading indicator for your future AI search citations.
4. **Lean into 3rd-Party Platforms:** Since AI bots favor Reddit, YouTube, and G2 for "objective" data, ensure your brand has a strong presence on these platforms.
5. **Play the Long Game:** Avoid "too-good-to-be-true" GEO hacks. The best approach is to be the brand that remains recommended *after* the AI companies finish their next round of spam-fighting updates.
